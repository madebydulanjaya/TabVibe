TabVibe Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

TabVibe is a Chrome extension that replaces your new tab page with a customizable background, search box, top sites, sticky notes, and optional news headlines.

What TabVibe does NOT do

  • TabVibe does not run any servers of its own.
  • TabVibe does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data, browsing history, or analytics with the developer or any third party.
  • TabVibe does not use tracking scripts, cookies, or third-party analytics SDKs.

What data TabVibe stores, and where

All of TabVibe's settings and sticky notes are stored using Chrome's built-in chrome.storage API, directly in your own browser:

  • Settings (background type, colors, filters, search engine, layout preferences, chosen news provider) are stored via chrome.storage.sync, which Chrome syncs across your own signed-in devices through your Google account. This sync is handled entirely by Google/Chrome — TabVibe never sees or transmits this data itself.
  • Sticky notes and cached photo/news data are stored via chrome.storage.local, kept only on your local device.

None of this data is accessible to the developer of TabVibe.

Network requests TabVibe makes

To provide its features, TabVibe's new tab page and background script make requests to:

  • Picsum Photos (picsum.photos) — to fetch a random background photo and its photographer attribution.
  • Your selected news source (e.g. BBC, The Guardian, TechCrunch, etc.) — only when you open the News panel or the extension refreshes cached headlines, and only to the single provider you have selected.
  • Search engine and news provider websites' favicon files, and Google's public favicon-lookup service as a fallback — solely to display each provider's icon.
  • Chrome's own internal favicon cache — to display icons for your most-visited sites.

These are standard HTTP requests; as with any website you visit, the receiving server can see your IP address and standard request headers. TabVibe does not add any additional tracking, identifiers, or analytics to these requests, and does not log or store the responses anywhere beyond your own browser's local cache.

Permissions TabVibe requests

  • storage — to save your settings and notes locally, as described above.
  • topSites — to show your most-visited sites as shortcuts on the new tab page.
  • alarms — to schedule automatic photo rotation at the interval you choose.
  • favicon — to display real site icons for your top sites using Chrome's local favicon cache.
  • Host permissions for Picsum Photos and the specific news provider domains listed above — solely to fetch photos and headlines as described.

Changes to this policy

If TabVibe's data practices change, this page will be updated accordingly.

Contact

Questions or concerns about this policy can be raised through the "Ask a question" link in TabVibe's Customize panel, which opens the extension's Chrome Web Store support page.
